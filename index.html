Instructions & Physics: Use the slider to move the two isolated atoms closer together. As distance decreases, their orbital wavefunctions begin to overlap. This coupling ($t$) splits the single degenerate atomic energy level ($\epsilon$) into a lower-energy bonding state ($E = \epsilon - t$) and a higher-energy antibonding state ($E = \epsilon + t$).

Instructions & Physics:
  • Increase N (Atoms): Watch the lattice populate from left to right. More atoms mean more wavefunctions overlapping, which increases the number of allowed energy states in the middle diagram.
  • Decrease Spacing (r): As atoms move closer, the coupling strength scales up sharply, causing the allowed energy levels to spread out vertically into a band. When they get close enough ($r < 3.0$), the states split into two separate groups: a lower Valence Band (green) and an upper Conduction Band (red), creating a forbidden Band Gap. This trajectory is tracked dynamically by the blue dashed indicator line on the right-hand Energy vs. Spacing graph.
